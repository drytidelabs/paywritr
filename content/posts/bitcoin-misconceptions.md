Every Thanksgiving dinner, someone brings up Bitcoin and the same myths come out. Let's address them once and for all.

<!--more-->

## 1. "Bitcoin is only used by criminals"

Cash is used by criminals far more than Bitcoin. The blockchain is a public ledger — every transaction is traceable. Law enforcement actually *loves* that criminals use Bitcoin because it leaves a permanent trail.

## 2. "It has no intrinsic value"

Neither does a $100 bill. Value comes from consensus, scarcity, and utility. Bitcoin has all three — plus it can't be printed by a central bank on a whim.

## 3. "It wastes energy"

Bitcoin mining increasingly uses stranded and renewable energy. The real question is: compared to what? The traditional banking system, with its offices, ATMs, armored trucks, and data centers, uses far more energy globally.

## 4. "It's too volatile to be useful"

Volatility is decreasing over time as adoption grows. And for people in countries with hyperinflation, Bitcoin's "volatility" looks like stability compared to their local currency.

## 5. "I missed the boat"

You don't have to buy a whole Bitcoin. You can buy 1,000 sats for a few cents. The boat hasn't left — most people haven't even heard of the dock.
